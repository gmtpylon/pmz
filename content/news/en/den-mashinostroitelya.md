On the last Sunday of September Russia celebrates Machine-Builder's Day — a holiday
for people who design, build and service the equipment industry runs on.
This year Pushkin Machine-Building Plant marked it on its own shop floor: certificates
went to design engineers, process technologists, CNC operators and NAKS-certified welders.

The certificates were handed out by the production director and representatives of the
Pushkin district of St. Petersburg. Separate thanks went to shop foremen responsible
for ground anchor system output and steel structures for special projects.

After the formal part — a tour of the production for employees' families: the rolling
mill, the heat-treatment area and the thermal-diffusion galvanizing line.
Machine-building in Pushkin is not just volume numbers, but the people who hold
those volumes in their hands every day.
