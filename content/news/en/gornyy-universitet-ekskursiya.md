In July the Pushkin Metallurgical Plant hosted a tour for first-year students of
the St. Petersburg Mining University. The future mining engineers came to the plant
as part of their summer practical training.

The visitors were shown the main production areas — from rolling and machining to the
manufacture of ground anchor systems and drilling tools. The plant's engineers
explained how the theory students learn at university turns into finished products
for hydraulic, road and special construction.

Visits like this are part of the plant's work with specialised universities: the
company is interested in a steady inflow of young specialists and regularly takes
students on for practical training.
